My Journey

My journey through high school was filled with ups and downs, especially during the challenging times brought by the COVID-19 pandemic. Despite the difficulties, I stayed committed to my goals and pushed myself to stay productive and focused. In my SPM, I proudly achieved outstanding results — 9As, including an A+, 3As, and 2A-. I was also honoured with the Best Subject Award for Biology. It wasn’t an easy path, but my dedication and perseverance kept me going. I’m deeply grateful to my teachers and friends, whose constant support and encouragement helped me through the toughest moments. Without them, I wouldn’t have made it this far.

During my matriculation studies, I took Biology, Mathematics, Chemistry, and Computer Science. It was my first time learning Computer Science, and I instantly fell in love with it. That experience sparked my passion and led me to pursue a degree in Software Engineering. Achieving a perfect 4.00 CGPA in both semesters was no easy feat, especially with the packed and demanding schedule. However, with discipline, resilience, and a genuine interest in what I was learning, I managed to overcome the challenges and stay on top of my goals.

As I progressed into my degree in Software Engineering, I had the opportunity to explore the world of computing in much greater depth. From learning core programming concepts and software development methodologies to understanding system design, databases, and human-computer interaction, this course has provided me with a comprehensive understanding of how software is built, maintained, and optimised.

Currently in Semester 4, I have maintained an excellent academic performance, with a CGPA of 3.965. I’m proud of this achievement, especially as I’ve been applying what I’ve learned in real-life scenarios, such as academic projects and personal development initiatives. The hands-on experience and practical knowledge I’ve gained so far have strengthened my passion for this field and continue to inspire me to grow as a future software engineer.
